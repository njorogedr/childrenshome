Who we are

Why Foster Care Matters

Founded in 2009, Every child deserves a loving, stable home where they can grow, learn, and reach their full potential. Foster care provides a vital safety net for children who cannot remain with their birth families due to circumstances beyond their control. At St. Dominic Foster Home, we are committed to creating an environment where children feel valued, protected, and empowered to build bright futures. Our comprehensive approach includes not only providing safe homes but also offering emotional support, educational assistance, and therapeutic services to help children heal from past trauma and develop the skills they need for successful adulthood.

Services

Our Foster Care Services

At St. Dominic Foster Home, we are more than just a shelter; we are a family. Our mission is to provide a nurturing environment where every child feels loved, respected, and empowered to achieve their dreams.

Foster Family Placement Club

We carefully match children with loving foster families who provide temporary or long-term care in a home environment.

Educational Support

We provide educational resources and support to help children succeed academically and prepare for their future.

Counseling & Therapy

We offer individual and group counseling sessions to help children process their experiences and develop coping strategies. Our licensed therapists specialize in trauma-informed care and work closely with each child to create a safe and supportive environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about fostering, our services, or how you can help? Find answers to common questions about fostering, the application process, and support services at St. Dominic Foster Care.

What are your opening hours?
We're open from 6:00AM to 10:00PM, Monday through Sunday.
What are the requirements to become a foster parent?
Foster parents must be at least 21 years old, have stable income and housing, pass background checks, complete training, and provide a safe, nurturing environment.
How long do children typically stay in foster care?
It varies—some children stay for a few weeks, while others may remain in foster care for several years. Our goal is always to work toward the best permanent solution for each child.
What support is provided to foster families?
We offer financial assistance, 24/7 support, training, counseling, educational advocacy, and case management.
Can foster children maintain contact with their birth families?
Yes! We support supervised visits, calls, and connections to maintain family bonds and emotional stability.
What happens when foster children turn 18?
We help with housing, education, employment, and life skills. Support often extends through age 21 or beyond.
